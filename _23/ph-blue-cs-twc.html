The White Company Case Study

innit

Overview

Summary

A list of set requirements from the business were to be accomplished, in addition to the request for an improved user experience overall.

Role and Duration

I was the Lead UX consultant for this optimisation project, working alongside the Product Manager, reporting into the Head of Digital. I was responsible for conducting research, redesigning detail and listing pages for various product types, and improving the checkout experience.

Details

Scope & Constraints

At the time, the company had no UX resources; only a visual design team who maintained the BAU work. The website was to be replatformed and would be transitioning from separate desktop & mobile entities, to a single responsive experience. This was a key consideration with each and every idea, proposal and outcome.

Goals

- Enhance aesthetic.
- Improve usability.
- Add features requested by biz.
- Deliver optimal mobile (responsive) experience.

Challenges

- Single resource (me).
- Educating Senior Stakeholders.
- Lack of reliable analytical data and opportunity for user testing.
- Strong visual identity to be maintained; loyal customer base to appease.
- Expansive range of product types with varying degrees of specification and properties.
- Responsive design mindset.
- Staggered approach to rollout.

Hypothesis

Less is more. Let the high-end products speak for themselves. Don’t overwhelm the user with incessant marketing bumf. Gently guide them through the product journey and be smart about cross-sell opportunities (smarter not louder). Many users are repeat customers; have the detailed info to hand and accessible, without forcing it on them. Let them add to bag in simple steps, and ensure a straightforward seamless checkout experience.

Process

Discovery

Benchmark reports, competitor analysis and direct customer feedback helped establish pain points and frustrations with the existing page layouts and designs. Addressing these issues whilst conceptualising new functional elements and features enabled a holistic approach; resulting in a more fluid, organic outcome.

Design

After analysing the existing page structures & elements, product specifications, and marketing/merchandising requirements, mid-fidelity mockups were created and iterated per stakeholder feedback.

Once the overall compositions were agreed, high-fidelity mockups were produced to test real content and aesthetic integration with the rest of the site.

Click-through InVision prototypes were assembled to outline linear user flows, which could be quickly altered on the fly. Hard-coded UI component prototypes were invaluable for demonstrating tangible interactions & responses; facilitating stakeholder comprehension and approval.

Outcome

Results/Feedback

It got implemented, albeit 18 months after I left. And, several years later, it’s still mostly my designs (they changed from fixed-width to full width, as I guess they rolled-out to the other BU cat’s)

Ticked-off biz req’s: ratings/reviews, ‘get the look’, ‘recommended products’, responsive design, quick view, portrait photos for clothing, maintained brand, added checkout options (collect+, international, gift boxing, gift vouchers)

After launch, the website reached the top 50 of the UK Retail Index report blah Top 500 (from top 150 previously).

TWC profits continued to rise - even during the pandemic, when their stores were forced to close.

Positive comments/evaluation found online.
