Samsung Case Study

innit

Overview

Role and Duration

I was assigned as Senior UX Design resource on this project to create an exclusive customer loyalty experience.

Scope & Constraints

The loyalty programme was a first for Samsung UK. As it was exclusive to S21 device owners, most of the experience would be mobile-based - however, accommodation of a desktop discovery/entry point was essential.

Many technical specifications remained unknown until late into the project, delaying finalisation of the optimal user flow. The loyalty rewards were partner offers, which were redeemed through an iframe launched within Samsung’s own ‘My Account’ platform. The redemption journeys differed between partners; ranging from promo code entry, to tokenised signup, to hard-coded affiliated app download links.

Challenges

Access to the loyalty offers was subject to device verification (S21 smartphone), and being logged-in to a Samsung user account. Qualified users would be directed to an offer overview/listing page, displaying cards that link to distinct offer detail pages.

Ineligible users would be presented with relevant messaging and relative signposting, based on their circumstance, as well as some aspirational marketing content for the scheme.

Landing pages needed an informational/aspirational balance, incorporating a “triage” process to identify various factors and direct the visitor accordingly. In any scenario, my objective was for any user to feel enticed, empowered, and enabled; not confused, dejected, and blocked.
